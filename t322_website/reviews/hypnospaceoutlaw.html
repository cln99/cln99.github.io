Hypnospace Outlaw

Hypnospace Outlaw is a game released in 2019, it simulates being a moderator on a late 90's internet forum. The game takes place in an alternate reality 1999 where "sleep-computing" is a thing, essentially you go to sleep and can use a computer system called HypnOS. From this you can access Hypnospace, which is essentially just a forum with a few different sections.

You play as a volunteer "Enforcer" designated to find anything that breaks the Hypnospace guidelines. You go around solving cases that are assigned to you, they can vary quite a bit, being about cyberbullying, illegal commerce, malicious software and various other topics.

Solving cases and figuring stuff out is really fun, the puzzles fit a good difficulty not being too easy or too hard and I personally really enjoyed slowly putting things together in some of the later cases. I particularly liked a section where you need to access a secret file hosting site and require a password to get past. You can either work out someone elses password or pay someone to give you one. This allows you to skip past the puzzle incase you can't figure out how to get the password.

After each case you get paid in "Hypnocoin", a currency which can only be used on Hypnospace. You can use this to buy a bunch of different things, music, virtual pets, stickers, games and a ton of other stuff.

The amount of content in Hypnospace is immense, there is a ton of stuff to find and I often found myself just exploring and reading all the different pages. All of the pages are made in a style reminicent of late 90's internet, along with eccentric and unique personalities which make for a really interesting and fun experience.

The game is divided into a few different chapters seperated by time skips over a few months. After each timeskip pages update letting you see how certain characters develop and every time skip I found myself checking back on my favourites to see what they were up to.

With all of these pages is a ton of music, all of which fit the pages really well. A lot of them have this oldschool MIDI feel to them that I really enjoy while some are just good original music. There are a lot of songs with Hypnospace boasting over 200 tracks. I'll put a few of my favourites here.

The overall story is pretty well done and goes in a way you wouldn't really expect, it doesn't get in the way of exploring Hypnospace and you can go at your own pace throughout. I do really like the story and the ending is very well done.

There is also modding support, a built in page creator and music sequencer in included allowing you to make your own pages and experiences on Hypnospace.

In conclusion, I really can't put into words how much I love this game, at times I feel like it was made specifically to fit my interest and love for oldschool internet. It simulates everything about old internet culture perfectly in a way I haven't seen anywhere else and I would highly recommend to those interested.
