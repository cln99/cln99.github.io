ULTRAKILL

ULTRAKILL is an high energy fast paced FPS inspired by games like Quake and Doom, I played ULTRAKILL about a year ago but replayed it recently and felt it was good for a review.

ULTRAKILL plays fast, it follows general FPS mechanics and adds some basic movement techniques like sliding, dash boosting and wall jumping that all work together really nicely to make an enjoyable movement system with a lot of depth. Along with this is several twists to the core FPS experience, most notably the fact that in order to heal yourself you need blood gained from damaging enemies up close, this encourages the player to get up close in order to stay alive which makes gameplay a lot more about dodging and circling enemies rather than hiding behind cover or waiting on enemies.

The weapon selection at first may seem simple and rudimentary but there's a real depth to each weapon and they are all used differently, and I found myself taking advantage of each of them throughout the game. There's a pistol with a basic chargeshot, a shotgun with an explosive grenade shot, a minigun type weapon with magnetic spikes and the railcannon which fires an extremely powerful laser shot. Each weapon also has a variant which wildly changes up how they're used, for instance, the pistol has a Marksman variant which replaces the chargeshot with the ability to throw a coin, any bullet that hits the coin gets reflected into the nearest enemy making it an incredibly useful and fun tool.

The enemy variety is nice as well with a varied cast, there are mindless melee gremlins, projectile shooting demons, high damage but low movement floating heads, flamethrower wielding enemies that can dodge attacks and various others. The bosses are also very inventive usually testing a specific skill of the players, the Corpse of King Minos for instance is a massive boss which throws slow but powerful punches to test the players spatial awareness. Something I really like about the bosses is that some of them end up becoming enemies that show up often in later levels which really makes the player feel a sense of progression.

Level design wise the game also hits hard with interesting designs and mechanics introduced throughout each of them, while most levels are quite linear they always feel unique and never end up feeling samey or boring, neither do they end up confusing as the game does a good job pointing the player to where they need to go. They also contain plenty of secret areas for the player to find which encourages a lot of exploration.

I feel it's worth mentioning that ULTRAKILL also has secret missions which tend to have completly unique gameplay made just for them. For instance, one of the secret missions, turns the game into a puzzle game inspired by "The Witness" and another turns it into a 3D platformer inspired by Crash Bandicoot. These are always fun and I found myself wondering what I would end up playing next when discovering them.

The music and graphics are also quite nice, the graphics follow a PS1 styled aesthetic even allowing the player to enable downscaling and dithering effects to further simulate the PS1 style. The music is good and fits the game very well being metalish breakcore (something like that at least). I really enjoyed the soundtrack and it works great in the game, keeping your heart pounding and adrenaline pumping.

Overall ULTRAKILL's gameplay is incredibly fun with a myriad of techniques and skills to learn and master, the enemies are all interesting and pose unique challenges. The weapon selection is simple but each weapon stays unique and I never found myself underusing any particular one. The bosses are interesting and the twists and restrictions they put on the player make for excellent challenges.

ULTRAKILL has easily one of my favorite (and most played) FPS games recently, I have played through the game about 3 times and gotten a P rank on every level, I would highly recommend to those who like movement/fast paced shooters.
