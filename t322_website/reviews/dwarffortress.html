Dwarf Fortress

Dwarf Fortress is a simulation game developed by Bay12Games, which consists of two brothers Tarn and Zach Adams. It started development in 2002 and has kept going ever since. Dwarf Fortress is easily the most complex and I suppose cutting edge is the way to describe it, it is the forefront of procedural generation in games. There is an entire fantasy world generator that generates not just a map, but towns, people, beasts, all in excruciating detail. Every person has a detailed personality and thoughts allowing them to act on their own, every fight has a detailed description on what body part is being attacked, what kind of damage was dealt and anything else that matters.

I began playing it around 2018-2019 and began learning how to play, I didn't really get into it the first few times I played but a few months later I decided to play it again and managed to learn most of the basics. After this I really got into it and it became one of my favourite games of all time.

Dwarf Fortress has three different modes, Fortress, Adventurer and Legends.

Fortress mode has you indirectly control a group of dwarves and create a wealthy and powerful fortress. Adventurer lets you control a singular adventurer who can go around the world, talking to people, going on quests and plenty of other activities. Legends lets you view the history of a world and any historical event including what you've done in Fortress and Adventurer.

Before you start playing you need to generate a world, you're given plenty of customization on world size, length of history, amount of sites and various other options to tweak the world to your liking. After the worldgen finishes you can pick any of the three modes to explore it.

An example of a generated world.

Dwarf Fortress Mode

Fortress mode is a simulation/management game about making a wealthy and powerful dwarven fortress. It is incredibly open-ended with there being no endgame or win condition along with a lot of freedom to do what you want.

Whatever you can think of doing, it's probably possible some way or another. Dwarf Fortress is easily the most open ended game I have ever played and I can think of very few situations where you are limited. If you want to flood the outside world with lava and hide in your fort, create a powerful militia to kill a group near your fort or try to tame ancient beasts to unleash on the elves, it's possible.

Another thing Dwarf Fortress does incredibly well is detail, every dwarf has a paragraph long personality description, plenty of thoughts and feelings and every dwarf acts differently to different situations. Every battle has a long page of every action being taken by the combatants and really gets your imagination going.

The gameplay itself is mostly ordering your dwarves around to get stuff done like mining down for ores, building stuff in workshops, farming and pretty much anything else you can think of. As someone who is only an intermediate when it comes to this game, some of the more complicated economies and crafts make my head spin but they all have a purpose whether it be making clothing, soap or honey.

Another thing to note that you wouldn't really expect from this kind of game is the music, it's a single four minute acoustic guitar track and it is incredible. It's a chaotic yet controlled track that perfectly fits the day to day management of your fortress. I can't put into words how much I love this track and how much it adds to the atmosphere of the game. You can listen to it here.

All of this complexity and freedom comes with a cost however. Starting out with Dwarf Fortress is really difficult, the UI can be confusing and overwhelming, the lack of control can make it confusing when dwarves simply don't do what they're told for whatever reason and the text based graphics all work together to make it difficult to start out.

However I believe that if you can stick with it and learn how to play, Fortress mode offers one of the most replayable, entertaining and mechanically deep games I've ever played.

Adventurer Mode

Adventure mode is an open ended, turn-based roguelike where you can explore the world, go on quests, kill bandits and whatever else you can think of. Despite Dwarf Fortress being the main mode, Adventurer is still pretty fleshed out with a lot going for it.

You have a lot of choices when it comes to making your character with completely customizable race, skills, personality, equipment and pets. There are a lot of options in all of these sections, there are a TON of different skills, a big table of values and beliefs to decide a personality, plenty of equipment and a lot of options for pets.

Unlike Fortress mode, your character can have an ultimate goal to attain whether it be something specific like raising a family, mastering a skill or something more vague like bathing the world in chaos. Following these goals and meeting your characters needs is important as neglecting these will make your character unfocused and damages their abilities.

After creating your character and choosing your location, you normally want to ask around for quests or trouble brewing around your area, which come in a few different types, you have bandit hideouts you can clear out, criminal organizations, thieving kobolds and hunting beasts which can range from a werelion to a titan made of platinum.

Most of these involve combat of some sort which brings me onto probably my favourite part of adventurer mode, which is its combat. Combat is fully anatomical, each attack is done on a specific part of the body and you can attack in various different ways, with a sword you can either slash, stab or make a blunt strike, if you don't have a weapon you can wrestle, punch, kick or bite your opponent.

This kind of system allows a lot of unique strategy and creative manuevers like damaging someone's legs to prevent them from retreating, poking their eyes to blind them or attacking an already wounded body part to stun the enemy. This all adds up into a really interesting and fun combat system that I haven't really seen elsewhere.

This also makes damage in fights a lot more long lasting and important as for instance if your legs get damaged in a fight, even if you win it'll be difficult to get somewhere safe. If you're a swordsman and your main arm gets cut off, you'll need to figure out a new way of combat.

If there's a weakpoint of adventurer, the stories and lore of the world aren't particularly interesting and talking to people tends to give you a bunch of cookie cutter responses that don't really mean much. This is something that will be improved in the future and the other parts of adventurer more than make up for it for me.

Conclusion

Dwarf Fortress is one of the most interesting games I have ever played and find it endlessly fascinating, it is very much its own thing with no other game really coming close to its detail, freedom and sheer amount of content. I can't wait to see where this game will end up in a decade or so and expect to continue playing it on and off for a long time.
