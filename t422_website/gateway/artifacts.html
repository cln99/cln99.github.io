TERM 1-2 ~ NON EXUBERANT THIRDS

At the start of Term 1 I wanted to make a 3D game, I decided on making a first person shooter called NX3. I didn't plan much about it or have a key idea to structure around but I started working on it.

I started just getting the bare bones of an FPS with a basic player controller, weapons and targets. At this point I had the idea to make a game similar to Binding of Isaac about going around randomly generated maps collecting items that change your stats and create "builds".

I also started using TrenchBroom, mapping software for Quake that is compatible with Godot. I started making a hub level based off a mansion and wanted to have it be the pause from the rest of the game where the player could buy items, heal or gamble.

This area is actually pretty well done in my opinion, there's a decent amount of stuff there including a whole minigame I had made during the holidays and while it is heavily rigged the slot machine is good fun too.

Onto the "actual" game, the best way I thought of doing randomly generated levels was to take a grid and generate a bunch of rooms then connect them up. The way I did this while it worked I wasn't able to figure out how to get rooms bigger than one square in the grid, so each room was fairly small meaning I couldn't add much in the or do anything that interesting.

I decided to make the game about going to each corner of the map which meant you had to travel nearly the whole map, which wasn't really fun or enjoyable due to how boring and repetitive the rooms would be.

There was also different floors added but this was really annoying to add and making stairs connect to rooms above was a nightmare which in the latest version of the game often breaks and tends to make stairs that go right into a ceiling.

The actual "game" part of NX3 was atrocious and unfun since I wasn't able to implement what I really wanted and what I ended up implementing instead just wasn't fun.

The graphics and models weren't too good either, the textures were mostly just stripes and whatever I could conjure up at the time. The models weren't really much of anything and while some of the mansion furniture and what not looks alright the enemies are just weird blocks.

There were a ton of random features and stuff I added like drinks, bosses and powerups that never really amounted to anything and a lot of ideas that just got scrapped due to being too big for me. I originally wanted the game to be about going to three seperate areas with different themes like nature, technology and death and to run through them like a typical FPS level (that's why it would be called NON EXUBERANT THIRDS) but I wasn't able to pull that off technically and had to scrap it.

Overall, NX3 was a big mess and while it did teach me a lot about game development and the pits and mistakes you can make, I feel it was a waste of two terms worth of work on a game that never ended up having any real gameplay.
